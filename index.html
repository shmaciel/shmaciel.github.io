I'm a Phd student at the University of Washington at Seattle. I obtained my Bachelors degree in Mathematics last year at the University of São Paulo (USP) in Brazil. My senior thesis was on the theory of motives, supervised by Olivier Martin from IMPA.

I am broadly interested in Algebraic Geometry, Algebraic Topology, Category Theory and Homological Algebra, especially the interaction between these areas.
In Algebraic Geometry, I am most interested in K-theory, Moduli Spaces, and Hodge Theory. In Algebraic Topology, my interests revolve around Homotopical Algebra and Stable Homotopy Theory, as well as applications to other areas, like Motivic Homotopy Theory, Derived Algebraic Geometry.
For most of my undergraduate studies, I was part of the organization of a student-run group of seminars at the University of São Paulo: the S4 Group.
